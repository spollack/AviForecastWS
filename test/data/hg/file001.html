Avalanche bulletin for the Chic-Chocs

Area concerned:Mont Albert, mont Hog’s Back, Champs-de-Mars, mont Lyall, mont Vallières-de-Saint-Réal, mont Blanche-Lamontagne and Mines-Madeleine.

Issued on : Saturday December 6th 2014 at 7:30

Avalanche problem #1 : Wind slabs

The hard wind slabs formed this week in the alpine on the N-NE-E slopes probably acquired greater stability over time, but we could not verify it. They nevertheless rely on or near the December 1st crust and also that of November 26th, and this is worrying. These surfaces provide a shear plane that could potentially initiate an avalanche. In addition, the new snow forecasted for the weekend could add more stress. With a MODERATE rating, it is possible for a skier, snowboarder or snowshoer to trigger a size 2 avalanche.

Avalanche activity

Our first observations of slab avalanches signs (size 2) took place at Mount Albert under the Mur des Patrouilleurs cornices during our visit on Friday. Your observations are essential to write avalanche bulletins, so do not hesitate to share them with us at info@centreavalanche.qc.ca or with the online form available on our main page.

Snowpack :

The Chic-Chocs received between 10 and 15cm of new snow since Wednesday morning. Extreme to strong winds blowing from the South first and then West, redistributing snow on the slopes N-NE-E areas of the treeline and alpine. The thin crust of December 1st is now covered with about 10 cm, but still perceptible under the skis. A hard base, the substantial crust formed on November 24th, is between 35 and 40 cm below the surface. In general, the snowpack varies from 50 to 70cm depending on elevation and aspects. CONDITIONS OF TRAVEL MAY BE DIFFICULT BECAUSE OF THE THIN EARLY SEASON SNOW COVER.

Weather forecast:

A storm in northern Quebec will bring 3-5cm of snow on Saturday with moderate S winds and temperatures will increase to -5C at noon. Low snowfall are possible in the night and Sunday with a significant temperatures cooling (max -18C) and strong Northwest winds.

Message:

Several Avalanche Skills Training courses are offered in English this winter. For more information, visit our website.
