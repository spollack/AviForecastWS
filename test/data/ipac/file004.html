Avalanche Advisory, February 22nd, 2013

02/22/2013

0 Comments

 
Good morning this is Eric Morgan with the avalanche advisory for today, February 22, 2013.  This report does not apply to local ski areas within the forecast region and the hazard rating will remain valid through midnight February 22, 2013. Special thanks to Idaho Parks and Recreation for sponsoring this morning's advisory.  Thanks also to Schweitzer Mountain Ski Patrol for observations from the mountain and Scott Rulander for assisting with the avalanche hunting yesterday.  

Weather 
Today, NWS is calling for 4-8 inches of new snow!!! 26 degrees, and W winds at 10-15 increasing to 24 with 37mph gusts .  Tonight,  will be mostly snowy with temperatures dropping to the upper teens and SW winds up to 30mph, picking up to 4 more inches, possibly more in the Cabinets.  This seems to be bringing in a warm moist system for Saturday.  The outlook for the weekend calls for 70% chance of a few more inches of new snow Saturday with high temperatures around 26 degrees and continued W'erly winds in the 20mph range.  Saturday night snowfall tapers to 1 inch possible, temperatures down to 20 with winds subsiding. Sunday weather continues to be mostly cloudy and cool, less winds and maybe another 1/2 inch of snow.  Avalanche danger will increase due to new snow and windloading for the outlook period.

Snowpack and Avalanche Discussion 
Yesterday Scott and I headed into the sidecountry out of Schweitzer expecting to see Low hazard conditions, stuck our head in the snow, once on accident, and what did we find....but a couple layers of Surface Hoar!!! about a foot and a half deep, failed with an ECP18 Q1 and CTM16 Q1, moderate force, but shears fast and clean.  There was another surface hoar layer about 4 inches down that did not react....Yet!!!!  I am concerned with the new snow and the windloading taking place and the stability of those surface hoar layers today and Saturday.  There is alot of variability in the snowpack and the distribution of the surface hoar, but I found it on a NW aspect and I am sure it is to be found elsewhere where slopes are sheltered from the wind and sun.  We had a RB of 4 with lots of energy on a 40 degree slope.  New snow will be loading onto the weak layers identified and will require the snowgoer to be on their game.  The avalanche hazard rating for the Selkirk and Cabinet Mountains is rated as Considerable  on slopes steeper than 35 degrees above 5,000 feet.  Avalanche hazard is rated as Moderate on slopes less than 35 degrees.

As we should all be aware....Avalanche conditions change for better or worse continually. Backcountry travelers should be prepared to assess current conditions for themselves, plan their routes of travel accordingly, and never travel alone. Backcountry travelers can reduce their exposure to avalanche hazards by utilizing timbered trails and ridge routes and by avoiding open and exposed terrain with slope angles of 30 degrees or more. Backcountry travelers should carry the necessary avalanche rescue equipment such as a shovel, avalanche probe or probe ski poles, a rescue beacon and a well-equipped first aid kit.

Have a safe and pleasant weekend.

Avalanche Advisory, February 15th, 2013

_Good morning this is Kevin Davis with the avalanche advisory for today, February 15, 2013.  This report does not apply to local ski areas within the forecast region and the hazard rating will remain valid through midnight February 15, 2013. Special thanks to Idaho Parks and Recreation for sponsoring this morning's advisory.  Thanks also to Schweitzer Mountain Ski Patrol for observations from the mountain and Gary Quinn for assisting with the avalanche hunting yesterday.  

Weather 
Today, NWS is calling for partly sunny skies in the mountains, 32 degrees, and W winds at 6mph .  Tonight,  will be mostly cloudy with temperatures dropping to the mid-20s and light S winds.  This seems to be bringing in a warm moist system for Saturday.  The outlook for the weekend calls for 100% chance of 1-3 inches of new snow Saturday with high temperatures around 33 degrees and SW winds at 8-15mph.  Saturday evening snowfall abates with an additional 1 inch possible, temperatures down to 18 degrees and W winds blowing at a steady 14mph with gusts to 25mph. Sunday weather continues but milder with temperatures in the low 30s and W winds calming to 10mph.  President's day could be partly sunny.   Avalanche danger will increase due to new snow and windloading for the outlook period.

Snowpack and Avalanche Discussion 
Yesterday Gary Quinn and I headed up Lightning Creek from Clark Fork and rode up the Wellington Creek Road to Round Top.  Southerly aspects were variable with breakable crust with surface hoar over the top on exposed slopes and a consolidated, firm layer of snow with sugary snow on top in sheltered areas.  Several crusts in the upper foot of snow from last week were giving the snow good stability but marginal for traveling.  On the NW aspect we found about the same depth of new snow in the last 2 weeks, good bond at the interface with the old snow surface, and good settlement in the upper pack.  What you'll want to be aware of is the bond of the new snow to the surface that is firm on southerly aspects and has surface hoar on sheltered and northerly aspects.  New snow will come in warm so look for possible weak layers in faceted snow above the crusts.  The avalanche hazard rating for the Selkirk and Cabinet Mountains is rated as MODERATE slopes steeper than 35 degrees above 5,000 feet.  Avalanche hazard is rated as LOW on slopes less than 35 degrees.

Weather

Snowpack and Avalanche Discussion

Weather 
Today, NWS is calling for partly sunny skies in the mountains with West wind 10-14 mph.  Temperatures are expected to rise to 31.  Tonight,  temperatures dropping to the low-20s with light SW winds.  The outlook for the weekend calls for sunny skies Saturday with temperatures at the freezing level becoming partly cloudy Sunday with high 27 degrees and SW winds 7-9 mph.   Avalanche danger will remain the same for the outlook period.

Snowpack and Avalanche Discussion

The avalanche hazard rating for the Selkirk and Cabinet Mountains is rated as Moderate on on all aspects steeper than 35 degrees.  Avalanche hazard is rated as LOW on slopes less than 35 degrees

Avalanche Advisory, January 25th, 2013

01/25/2013

0 Comments

 
_Good morning this is Kevin Davis with the avalanche advisory for today, January 25th, 2013.  This report does not apply to local ski areas within the forecast region and the hazard rating will remain valid through midnight January 25th, 2013.  Thanks to Tom Eddy and Schweitzer Mountain Patrol for the numerous observations on snowpack and pits this week.  Thanks to Brandon Glaza for assisting with pit digging in the Cabinets yesterday. 

Weather 
Change in the weather.  Today, NWS is calling for a 70% chance of 1-3 inches of new snow.  Temperatures are expected to rise to 31, but at 6AM snotel sites in the Cabinets and Selkirks are already hovering at that mark.  Tonight, a 90% chance of 3-5 inches with temperatures dropping to the mid-20s and SW winds at 7-9mph.  The outlook for the weekend calls for continuing snowfall with 2-4 inches likely on Saturday, 1-3 more possible Saturday evening, and more on Sunday.  Temperatures will be decreasing during this period of time and winds will remain SWerly. Avalanche danger will increase on some aspects with the expected weather for the outlook period.

Snowpack and Avalanche Discussion 
Settlement has been the rule for the snowpack up to Wednesday of this week.  The pack was firm on the surface due to wind and sun effect and mostly increasing in density with depth save a couple of lower density layers that sheared with moderate to hard force.  We were reporting mostly stable conditions on this pack.  Now this is buried and on most aspects yesterday we found that the new snow had fallen less dense and with good bonding to the surface.  We found some moderate shears on a change in density layer about 6-8 inches deep on a north aspect.  On a sheltered due east aspect on Lunch Peak we found unstable conditions with the new snow on buried surface hoar.  Compression tests and ECT tests revealed low scores on the surface hoar at 1 foot depth and with new snow last night, now deeper and possibly weaker.  This weak layer will exist on NE, E, and SE aspects.  With significant warming today, use caution on these slopes.  Colder temperatures this weekend will help stability, but windloading from consistent SW winds could be enough to keep things unstable on easterly aspects.

The avalanche hazard rating for the Selkirk and Cabinet Mountains is rated as CONSIDERABLE on NE, E, and SE aspects steeper than 30 degrees above 4, 500 feet.  Avalanche hazard is rated as MODERATE on steep slopes on all other aspects.

Avalanche Advisory, January 18th, 2013

01/18/2013

0 Comments

 
Good morning this is Kevin Davis with the avalanche advisory for today, January 18th, 2013.  This report does not apply to local ski areas within the forecast region and the hazard rating will remain valid through midnight January 18th, 2013.  Special thanks to Idaho Parks and Recreation for sponsoring this avalanche advisory.  Thanks to Scott Rulander for assisting with pit digging in the Selkirks yesterday. 

Weather 
Today will be partly sunny with temperatures in the mid 20s and light SW winds.  Tonight will be mostly cloudy, temperatures in the low 20s with continuing light SW winds. The outlook for Saturday and Sunday calls for partly sunny during the day and mostly cloudy at night.  Day-time temperatures increasing each day to a high of 36 on MLK day and winds remaining SW. Avalanche danger will likely remain the same through the outlook period.

Snowpack and Avalanche Discussion 
Yesterday we rode up Ruby Creek, nice job grooming guys, and toured the Upper and Lower Roman Nose Lakes.  We hit all aspects and found the surface snow conditions to be quite variable.  Mostly firm conditions out there due to wind scour and melt-freeze from the sun.  Our snow pits on NW and east aspects revealed a few layers around changes in density in the upper 2 feet of the pack but nothing is shearing easily or with energy.  Overall the snowpack is mostly stable with some isolated pockets of less stable snow near windscoured ridges on any aspect.  Watch the steeper, exposed terrain around ridges for firmer windslabs that could be touchy.  We found some nice snow in sheltered areas in the trees.  In the Cabinets the snow conditions are a little different with light fluffy snow over the firmer, consolidated snow from the last week.  The wind has not been as strong and the mild weather seems to have favored some lake effect snowfall that has produced some very low density snow.  Watch for waming temps to slab up and create some unstable snow, especially with direct sun.  Conditions could change by MLK day due to warming, especially on southerly aspects.

The avalanche hazard rating for the Selkirk and Cabinet Mountains is rated as LOW on all aspects with pockets of MODERATE on upper windloaded slopes greater than 35 degrees.

Avalanche Advisory, January 11th, 2013 

01/11/2013

1 Comment

 
Good morning this is Eric Morgan with the avalanche advisory for today, January 11th, 2013.  This report does not apply to local ski areas within the forecast region and the hazard rating will remain valid through midnight January 11th, 2013.  Special thanks to Idaho Parks and Recreation for sponsoring this avalanche advisory.  Thanks to Silver Mountain Ski Patrol and Schweitzer Mountain Patrol for their observations on weather and snowpack from the high country. 

Weather 
Partly sunny.  Temperatures down to 11 degrees with light Northeast winds 11-16mph.  Tonight continues with clear and cold temperatures down to 8 in the mountains.  Saturday will look more sunny with a high around 12 and light winds, 6mph out of the South.  The outlook for the weekend beyond Saturday calls the same.  Temperatures will hold in the low teens and single digits during the nights.  Avalanche danger will likely remain the same through the outlook period.

Snowpack and Avalanche Discussion 
Yesterday in the Cabinet Mountains, in the snowy weather we found good travel conditions and moderately stable conditions.  We found a rain and wind crust under a few inches of snow up to about 4500 feet where the melt freeze crust started to soften and get better as we climbed upward toward Lunch Peak.  With the recent snow events we found 3 inches of fluff on top of a 2 inch wind slab that sits above a light softer layer with a 4 finger hardness.  When that wind slab breaks loose it does so on that soft layer 6-8 inches below the surface.  This will be our concern for a while.  There is also another thin crust about 48 cm below the snow surface formed from last Thursday's sun.  Compression tests show shears with these wind slabs on the softer snow.  Where there is windloading on these slabs and more snow accumulation one should be heads up.  Keep an eye out for these layers in your pits and be a little more conservative if you are in wind affected or  loaded areas.

Total snow depth in both ranges was 8-9 feet. Be aware of wind loading that may have occurred on lee aspects that sits atop a wind hammered slab.  

The avalanche hazard rating for the Selkirk and Cabinet Mountains is rated as Moderate on all aspects with pockets of considerable on upper windloaded slopes greater than 35 degrees.

Avalanche Advisory, January 4th, 2013

01/04/2013

0 Comments

 
Good morning this is Joseph Akers with the avalanche advisory for today, January 4th, 2013.  This report does not apply to local ski areas within the forecast region and the hazard rating will remain valid through midnight January 4th, 2013.  Special thanks to Idaho Parks and Recreation for sponsoring this avalanche advisory.  Thanks to Silver Mountain Ski Patrol and Schweitzer Mountain Patrol for their observations on weather and snowpack from the high country. 

Weather

 A slight chance of flurries exists for today under mostly cloudy skies with no significant accumulation of new snow.  Temperatures will rise to the low 20s with light southwest winds.  Tonight continues with a slight chance of trace accumulations and temperatures dropping to the mid-teens and winds shifting to the south.  Saturday will look much the same with highs in the 20s, light winds and cloudy skies.  The outlook for the weekend beyond Saturday calls for an increasing chance of light snowfall Saturday evening through Monday with a couple inches expected each day.  Temperatures will hold with mid 20s during the day and mid teens through the nights.  Avalanche danger will likely decrease through Saturday but may increase with the expected fresh snow Sunday and Monday.

Snowpack and Avalanche Discussion

Yesterday In the Selkirk Mountains, under beautiful blue skies we found good travel conditions and mostly stable conditions.  Due to low snow accumulations from the previous week combined with moderate temperatures and low winds the old weak layers are settling and the snowpack is stabilizing nicely. Compression tests revealed two weaknesses that you should be looking out for.  First, at about ten inches down a layer of large stellar crystals that fell during the last storm cycle and secondly, further down at about the 3ft mark, a thin ice layer.  Keep an eye out for these layers in your pits and be a little more conservative if you are in wind affected/ loaded areas.

In the Cabinets it is much the same as the Selkirks.  Total snow depth in both ranges was 8-9 feet. Be aware of wind loading that may have occurred on lee aspects and wind scouring on the windward aspects.  There were large surface hoar crystals throughout the region yesterday morning but by mid afternoon that coveted sunshine had melted them down.  By the end of the day a minor sun crust was beginning to form on southerly aspects.

The avalanche hazard rating for the Selkirk and Cabinet Mountains is rated as LOW on all aspects.  The avalanche hazard is rated as MODERATE near ridge-tops in areas of wind-loading or wind-effected snow steeper than 35 degrees.

  Good morning this is Kevin Davis with the avalanche advisory for today, December 28th, 2012.  This report does not apply to local ski areas within the forecast region and the hazard rating will remain valid through midnight December 28th, 2012.  Special thanks to Idaho Parks and recreation for sponsoring this avalanche advisory.  Thanks to Silver Mountain Ski Patrol and Schweitzer Mountain Patrol for their observations on weather and snowpack from the high country.  A slight chance of flurries exists for today under mostly cloudy skies.  Temperatures in the low 20s and light west winds.  Tonight continues with a slight chance of trace accumulations and temperatures dropping to the mid-teens.  The outlook for the weekend calls for an increasing chance of light snowfall on Saturday with only an inch expected, temperatures in the low 20s and light SW winds.  Sunday will continue to be mild.  Avalanche danger will decrease with the expected weather for the outlook period.    
In the Cabinet Mountains yesterday Eric and I traveled around the Trestle Peak area and found mostly stable conditions due to moderate snow accumulations helping to settle old weak layers.  Time has also helped to stabilize the pack.  Some wind was moving light surface snow at the ridge-tops so you may find some slabs developed on lee aspects due to wind-loading and denser surface slabs on windward aspects.  In the Selkirks near Schweitzer Mountain compression tests revealed low quality collapses in near surface snow.  Minor wind slabs have developed near the ridge-tops.  Total snow depth in both ranges was 8-9 feet.  Depth to the December 4th ice crust weak layer was about 5 feet and was not reacting.  Log this old weak layer in your memory since it will deteriorate over time and could become a weak layer again.  The avalanche hazard rating for the Selkirk and Cabinet Mountains is rated as LOW on all aspects.  The avalanche hazard is rated as MODERATE near ridge-tops in areas of wind-loading or wind-effected snow steeper than 35 degrees.
