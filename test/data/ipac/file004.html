Avalanche Advisory, February 22nd 2013

02/22/2013

0 Comments

 
Good morning this is Eric Morgan with the avalanche advisory for today, February 22, 2013.  This report does not apply to local ski areas within the forecast region and the hazard rating will remain valid through midnight February 22, 2013. Special thanks to Idaho Parks and Recreation for sponsoring this morning's advisory.  Thanks to Ed, Carole and Dan for the observations down south. 

Weather 
Today, NWS is calling for 3-7 inches of new snow in the mountains!!! 32 degrees, and S/SW winds at 11-14mph with 37mph gusts .  Tonight, will be mostly snowy with temperatures dropping to the upper teens and SW winds 15-24mph with gusts to 50 picking up 5-9 more inches with a total of 1 foot by tomorrow.  The outlook for the weekend calls for 100% chance of a few more inches of new snow Saturday with high temperatures around 23 degrees and continued gusty west winds.  Saturday evening snowfall rate tapers with an additional 1 inch possible, temperatures down to 20 with winds subsiding. Sunday weather continues to be mostly cloudy and cool, less winds and maybe another 1/2 inch of snow.  Avalanche danger will increase due to new snow and windloading for the outlook period.

Snowpack and Avalanche Discussion 
Yesterday Ed was in the Roundtop Mountain area of the St. Joe (see profile above) and did not get much, but note there is buried surface hoar going to rounds in places but still there just over a foot below the surface.  This layer will be tested with the new snow that fell in the past 24 hours and the expected snow today.  Carol and Dan were in at Lookout Pass in 153 cm of snow at 6000 feet looking at last weeks surface crust under 6 inches which now has an additional 8 inches of fresh on it, and more in windloaded areas.  So, these will  be the layers to be aware of down in the CDA - St.Regis Basin areas.  The avalanche hazard rating for the Coeur d Alene and St. Joe Mountains is rated as CONSIDERABLE  on slopes steeper than 35 degrees above 5,000 feet.  Avalanche hazard is rated as Moderate on slopes less than 35 degrees.

As we should all be aware....Avalanche conditions change for better or worse continually. Backcountry travelers should be prepared to assess current conditions for themselves, plan their routes of travel accordingly, and never travel alone. Backcountry travelers can reduce their exposure to avalanche hazards by utilizing timbered trails and ridge routes and by avoiding open and exposed terrain with slope angles of 30 degrees or more. Backcountry travelers should carry the necessary avalanche
rescue equipment such as a shovel, avalanche probe or probe ski poles, a rescue beacon and a well-equipped first aid kit.

Have a safe and pleasant weekend.

Avalanche Advisory, February 15th, 2013

02/15/2013

0 Comments

 
_Good morning this is Kevin Davis with the avalanche advisory for today, February 15th, 2013.  This report does not apply to local ski areas within the forecast region and the hazard rating will remain valid through midnight February 15th, 2013. Special thanks to Idaho Parks and Recreation for sponsoring this morning's advisory.  Thanks also to Silver Mountain Ski Patrol for their pit data from the mountain earlier this week.  

Weather 
Today, the NWS is calling for mostly cloudy conditions, temperatures around 33 degrees and SW winds at 7mph.   Tonight will be partly cloudy with SW winds at 5-9mph and temperatures in the high-20s.  The outlook for the weekend calls for an 80% chance of 1-3 inches of new snow, 37 degrees, and SW winds at 11-15 with gusts to 24mph.  Snow continues Saturday night with a 100% chance of 1-3 more inches, temperatures in the low-20s and W winds continuing at 14-18mph and gusty. Sunday you'll likely see a little more snow, decreasing temperatures, but that W wind still a blowin.  Avalanche danger will increase for the outlook period.

Snowpack and Avalanche Discussion 
The big thing we will need to be aware of is the expected weather this weekend.  Dan and Carole found a marginal snowpack in the Bullion Pass area yesterday with moderately weak shears in the upper 1 foot at 5800 elevation.  They are tracking an old surface hoar layer that is 3 feet deep and on easterly aspects is shearing with some energy in their tests.  Dan said he had some concerns for the steeper terrain on easterly aspects.  Ed found the surface hoar layer to be gaining strength where he dug on Round Top but you might be careful on easterly aspects at the highest elevations.  On Silver Mountain, Patrol found shears at 10 inches deep in decomposing new snow and 1.5 feet on a thin crust.  The big three weather factors will be a concern this weekend - West winds, rising temps, and new snow.  Watch for the signs.  Rain is a possibility during the day Saturday.  Surface hoar may become buried on some aspects.  The avalanche hazard for the CDA and St. Joe Mountains is rated as MODERATE, with isolated pockets of Considerable avalanche danger, on east facing aspects steeper than 35 degrees above 5000 feet.  Avalanche hazard is rated as Low on all slopes less than 35 degrees.

The avalanche hazard rating for the CDA and St. Joe Mountains is rated as Moderate on on all aspects steeper than 35 degrees above 5000 feet with pockets of Cosiderable on the windloaded areas.  Avalanche hazard is rated as Low on all slopes less than 35 degrees.

Avalanche Advisory, January 25th, 2013

01/25/2013

0 Comments

 
_Good morning this is Kevin Davis with the avalanche advisory for today, January 25th, 2013.  This report does not apply to local ski areas within the forecast region and the hazard rating will remain valid through midnight January 25th, 2013.  Joel Kopf has been tracking up Grandmother Mountain on the St. Joe/Clearwater Divide so we thank him for the great data from that area.

Weather 
Big sluice of weather heading your way.  Today the NWS predicts a 100% chance of 2-4 inches of new snow and temperatures rising to 36 with winds out of the south at 8mph.  Tonight the snowfall continues with 3-5 more inches, temperatures dropping to 28 and south winds at 6-8mph.  The outlook for the weekend calls for more snow with an additional 1-3 inches Saturday, Saturday night, and Sunday.  That's a potential for 18 inches over 3 days.  Temperatures will be dropping over this period of time and SW persistent at 8mph.  Avalanche danger on the rise today and decreasing slightly for the outlook period.

Snowpack and Avalanche Discussion 
Ed was out in the Twin Saddle area yesterday out of Avery and found mostly stable conditions on south and west aspects and exposed slopes, but found buried surface hoar on easterly aspects and very pronounced on sheltered slopes.  Stability tests on this layer about 10 inches to one foot deep revealed significant weakness and potential for propagating a fracture.  Data from the St. Regis Basin yesterday showed mostly stable conditions with 4-6 inches of new snow bonded well to a firm pack.  Carole did not report finding buried surface hoar in the Basin but you might be looking for it in steep, sheltered terrain. 48 hour accumulations for Lost Lake snotel were 4-6 inches so expect more on lee aspects.  With temperatures on the rise today be very careful on steep easterly aspects.  With the possibility of rain today at higher elevations be aware that the instability of the weak surface hoar layer could increase further.  Temperatures decreasing over the weekend will help in the future to create a more stable situation with less dense snow on top but watch for consistent SW winds to be developing pockets of continued weak layers on lee aspects.

The avalanche hazard rating for the St. Regis Basin and St. Joe areas are rated as MODERATE NE, E, and SE aspects steeper than 30 degrees above 5,00 feet.  The avalanche hazard is rated as LOW on steep slopes on all other aspects.

Avalanche Advisory, January 18th, 2013

01/18/2013

0 Comments

 
Good morning this is Kevin Davis with the avalanche advisory for today, January 18th, 2013.  This report does not apply to local ski areas within the forecast region and the hazard rating will remain valid through midnight January 18th, 2013.  Special thanks Silver Mountain Ski Patrol for pit data and Joel Kopf for observations and pit data from Grandmother Mountain on the St. Joe/Clearwater Divide.

Weather 
Today you have mostly sunny skies and temperatures in the low 30s and SW winds 14-18mph with higher gusts.  Tonight will be mostly cloudy with temperatures in the mid 20s and moderate winds out of the west and gusts in the 20mph range.  The outlook for Saturday and Sunday calls for more sun meaning temperatures will be rising into the 30s and the SW winds blowing at 10-14mph.  Sunday, same thing with a little less wind.  By MLK day it will get quite warm so watch for south slopes to be heating up  Avalanche danger will likely remain the same through the outlook period.

Snowpack and Avalanche Discussion 
In the St. Regis Basin yesterday, Carole and Dan found that the wind has been actively building cornices with some large cornice development observed around Steven's Peak.  With a little over 1 foot of new snow in the last week you can expect some deeper deposits on lee aspects.  Carole felt these deposits in steeper terrain would be the main concern.  They also noted buried surface hoar on sheltered aspects that sheared cleanly but with moderate force in shear tests.  To the south Ed found a more stable pack with no major weak layers revealed in stability tests on Landmark Peak.  Joel Kopf also noted mainly stable conditions on Grandmother Mountain.  Sheltered terrain, such as in the St. Regis Basin, may be allowing deeper wind deposition and may be harboring isolated pockets of buried surface hoar.  Check the upper 2-3 feet of the pack if you feel you are in terrain that has not undergone stronger, scouring winds. 

The avalanche hazard rating for the St. Regis Basin and St. Joe areas are rated as Moderate on all aspects with pockets of CONSIDERABLE on upper windloaded slopes greater than 35 degrees.

Avalanche Advisory, January 4th, 2013

01/04/2013

0 Comments

 
Good morning this is Joseph Akers with the avalanche advisory for today, January 4th, 2013.  This report does not apply to local ski areas within the forecast region and the hazard rating will remain valid through midnight January 4th, 2013.  Special thanks to Idaho Parks and Recreation for sponsoring this avalanche advisory.  Thanks to Silver Mountain Ski Patrol and Joel Kopf for their observations on weather and snowpack from the high country. 

Weather

A slight chance of flurries exists for today under mostly cloudy skies with no significant accumulation of new snow.  Temperatures will rise to the upper 20s with light southwest winds.  Tonight continues with a slight chance of trace accumulations and temperatures dropping to the low 20s and winds shifting to the south.  Saturday will look much the same with highs in the 20s, light winds and cloudy skies.  The outlook for the weekend beyond Saturday calls for an increasing chance of light snowfall Saturday evening through Monday with a couple inches expected each day.  Temperatures will hold with upper 20s during the day and lower 20s through the nights.  Avalanche danger will likely decrease through Saturday but may increase with the expected fresh snow Sunday and Monday.

Snowpack and Avalanche Discussion

Carole and Dan were up at Lookout Pass yesterday and.  They measured about 4.5 feet of total snow depth at about 6000ft in a mostly stable snowpack.  They identified a melt freeze layer at about the 3 foot mark as their layer of most concern although it took a high amount of force to trigger any failure.  Overall the snowpack has settled and stabilized over the last week. They did have concerns for steeper slopes especially where wind-loaded.  

In the St. Joe region Ed Odegaard found snow depths between 165cm to 215cm depending on degree of wind loading.  Overall the stability was good. His greatest concern was a weak layer in the top couple of feet where wind loading had occurred.  The avalanche hazard rating for the Coeur d Alene and St. Joe's Mountains is rated as LOW on slopes less than 35 degrees.  The avalanche hazard is rated as MODERATE near ridge-tops in areas of wind-loading or wind-effected snow steeper than 35 degrees.
